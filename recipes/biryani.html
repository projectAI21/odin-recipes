Biryani

Description

Biryani is a flavorful, aromatic rice dish layered with spiced meat or vegetables and cooked to perfection. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, it's a festive dish loved for its rich taste and vibrant presentation.

Whether it's chicken, mutton, or veggie biryani, the slow cooking method infuses each grain of rice with flavor. It’s often served with raita or boiled eggs for a wholesome meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken (or your choice of protein)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Spices (turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala)
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • 4 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Cook until 70% done and set aside.
  2. In a pan, sauté onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, then tomatoes and spices. Cook till oil separates.
  4. Add chicken and yogurt. Cook until chicken is tender.
  5. Layer half the rice, then chicken mix, then the remaining rice.
  6. Top with fried onions, mint, and coriander. Cover and cook on low for 20 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with raita or boiled egg.
